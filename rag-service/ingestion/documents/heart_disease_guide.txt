Heart disease refers to conditions affecting the heart's structure and function.

Risk factors for heart disease include high blood pressure, high cholesterol, smoking, diabetes, obesity, physical inactivity, and family history.

Chest pain or discomfort is the most common symptom of heart disease. It may feel like pressure, squeezing, fullness, or pain in the center of the chest.

Other symptoms include shortness of breath, pain in the neck, jaw, throat, upper abdomen or back, and numbness or coldness in legs or arms.

High blood pressure (hypertension) makes the heart work harder and can lead to heart disease. Normal blood pressure is below 120/80 mmHg.

High cholesterol leads to plaque buildup in arteries. Total cholesterol above 200 mg/dL is considered borderline high.

Regular exercise for at least 30 minutes most days helps reduce heart disease risk significantly.

A heart-healthy diet includes fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and limits saturated fats, sodium, and added sugars.

Smoking doubles the risk of heart disease. Quitting smoking reduces risk significantly within one to two years.

Stress management through meditation, yoga, and adequate sleep can help reduce cardiovascular risk.

An ECG (electrocardiogram) measures electrical activity of the heart and helps detect abnormalities.

Thalassemia is a blood disorder that can affect heart function. People with thalassemia require regular cardiac monitoring.

Exercise-induced angina is chest pain that occurs during physical activity and indicates reduced blood flow to the heart.

ST depression on an ECG indicates reduced blood flow to the heart muscle during exercise.
